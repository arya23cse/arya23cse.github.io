Symptoms of Weak Mental Health

  • Excessive worrying or fear.
  • Feeling restless or on edge.
  • Difficulty controlling worrying thoughts.
  • Persistent sadness or feelings of emptiness.
  • Lack of interest in activities once enjoyed.
  • Changes in appetite or weight.
  • Feeling overwhelmed or constantly under pressure.
  • Changes in appetite or sleep patterns.
  • Irritability or anger.
  • Avoiding social situations or isolating oneself from others.
  • Changes in appetite or sleep patterns.
  • Feeling disconnected or detached from others.
  • Frequent irritability, anger, or outbursts of frustration.
  • Mood swings or emotional instability.
  • Difficulty managing emotions or feeling overwhelmed by them.
  • Persistent thoughts of self-harm, suicide, or death.
  • Feeling trapped, hopeless, or like a burden to others.
  • Expressing feelings of worthlessness or guilt.
  • Significant weight loss or gain that is not intentional.
  • Loss of interest in food or a noticeable increase in emotional eating.
  • Obsessive thoughts about body image or fear of weight gain.
  • Persistent feelings of tiredness or low energy levels.
  • Difficulty completing tasks or engaging in activities.
  • Lack of motivation or increased procrastination.
  • Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or experiencing restless sleep.
  • Frequent nightmares or night sweats.
  • Feeling tired or fatigued even after a full night's sleep.
